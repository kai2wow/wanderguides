Welcome to The Lodge at Sereno Del Mar

Welcome to The Lodge at Sereno Del Mar, a tranquil coastal retreat nestled just steps from Portuguese Beach in beautiful Bodega Bay. Set on half an acre and backing onto a sprawling 600-acre ranch, the home offers stunning views of rolling pastures and the ocean beyond, where the soothing sounds of waves greet you daily. Whether you’re enjoying coffee on the deck as cows wander by, exploring nearby hiking trails and beaches, or spotting migrating whales in the spring, this serene getaway is the perfect place to relax or embark on adventures to nearby destinations like Napa, Russian River, and San Francisco.

Purpose-Driven Travel Begins Here

As a guest at The Lodge at Sereno Del Mar, we invite you to give back to the local community by volunteering with one of the many organizations in the area. This guide will help you discover opportunities to make a positive impact during your stay.

Connect

Meet like-minded individuals and build relationships with the local community.

Ground

Impact

Make a meaningful difference in the lives of others by volunteering with organizations that support the local community.

Inspire

Be inspired by the dedication and passion of local organizations working to create positive change.

At WanderWell, we believe that travel and giving back go hand-in-hand. Our mission is to provide opportunities for guests to engage with local communities and make a positive impact on the places they visit.

Food For Thought Food Bank provides healthy, medically tailored food to neighbors in Sonoma County living with serious medical conditions.

Russian Riverkeeper

Verified
Who they are

Russian Riverkeeper advocates for clean water, protects wildlife, and works to preserve the Russian River watershed through community action and restoration projects.

What you'll do

Participate in cleanups such as removing trash from creeks, roads, and the Russian River, or removing invasive species to help restore habitats. Upcoming events include Santa Rosa Creek Cleanup, Colgan Creek Cleanup, and Earth Day Cleanup.
